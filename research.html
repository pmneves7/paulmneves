Research

Quantum materials, neutron scattering, and emergent phases of correlated matter.

Quantum materials

A central theme of my research is the discovery and characterization of quantum materials whose electrons interact strongly enough to produce unconventional magnetism, superconductivity, and other collective phases. I work with single crystals and thin films spanning correlated metals, frustrated magnets, and topological systems, combining transport, thermodynamic, and scattering measurements to connect microscopic Hamiltonians to macroscopic behavior. Much of this work emphasizes how crystal structure, dimensionality, and chemical tuning steer competing interactions and open windows into new physics.

Neutron scattering

Neutron scattering is my primary probe of magnetic order, excitations, and lattice dynamics in quantum materials. I use diffraction to determine magnetic structures and inelastic scattering to map spin waves, crystal-field excitations, and continuum response as a function of temperature, magnetic field, and pressure. These measurements are performed at national user facilities, where careful sample environment design and resolution planning are essential to isolate the signals of interest. I am also interested in how neutron techniques complement other probes such as ARPES, transport, and resonant spectroscopy to build a coherent picture of the underlying physics.

Emergent phenomena

I am motivated by emergent phenomena that arise when electrons organize in unexpected ways, including flat-band physics, strange metallicity, quantum criticality, and field-tuned superconductivity. These states often appear near phase boundaries where small changes in temperature, field, or composition produce dramatic responses. My goal is to identify the relevant degrees of freedom and symmetries that govern such behavior, and to understand when simple quasiparticle descriptions break down. This program connects fundamental condensed matter questions to materials platforms that may guide the design of future quantum technologies.
