Biography

Paul M. Neves is a Gordon and Betty Moore Fellow in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at Johns Hopkins University, where he works with Collin Broholm. He earned his B.S. in Physics from the University of Maryland, working with Nicholas Butch at the National Institute of Standards and Technology and UMD, and his Ph.D. from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology with Joe Checkelsky.

An experimental condensed matter physicist, Dr. Neves studies quantum materials using neutron scattering, spectroscopy, and complementary experimental probes. He is interested in the microscopic physics of correlated electrons, magnetism, superconductivity, and emergent phases of matter.

Dr. Neves was a Goldwater Scholar in 2018.
