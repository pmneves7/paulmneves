Biography

Paul M. Neves is a Gordon and Betty Moore Fellow in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at Johns Hopkins University, where he works with Collin Broholm. He earned his B.S. in Physics from the University of Maryland, College Park, working with Nicholas Butch at the National Institute of Standards and Technology Center for Neutron Research and UMD, and his Ph.D. from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology with Joe Checkelsky.

An experimental condensed matter physicist, Dr. Neves studies quantum materials using neutron scattering, spectroscopy, and complementary experimental probes. He is interested in the microscopic physics of correlated electrons, magnetism, superconductivity, and emergent phases of matter.

Dr. Neves was a National Merit Scholar in 2015, received a Banneker/Key Scholarship at UMD, was awarded a Goldwater Scholarship in 2018, and was an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship Honorable Mention in 2019 and 2021. He graduated summa cum laude with departmental high honors in physics.
